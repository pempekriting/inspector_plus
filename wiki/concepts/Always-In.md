# Always-In Direction

The most important concept in trading. Traders should constantly assess whether the market is trending, and an "always-in" approach helps make that determination.

## Definition

If you had to be in the market at all times, either long or short, the always-in position is whatever your current position is. Some variation of this is used by many institutions. Most mutual funds are usually always-in long. Most hedge funds remain close to fully invested.

## Application

1. **In a Bull Trend**: If always-in long in a bull trend, only look for longs OR wait for always-in flip to short before taking shorts
2. **In a Bear Trend**: If always-in short, only look for shorts OR wait for always-in flip to long before taking longs
3. **In a Trading Range**: If always-in position does not change, consider taking scalps only in the direction of the trend

## Always-In Flip Requirements

The minimum requirement for an always-in flip to short is usually that the follow-through bar not have a bull close. If it is a small bear bar or a doji bar, this confirms bears are in control for most traders.

If the follow-through bar has a strong bull body or is a bull reversal bar, this can be a buy signal for a failed breakout.

## Trend vs Trading Range

When a strong trend transitions into a trading range, the always-in position often remains unchanged. In this case, traders should scalp most or all of their trades in the direction of the trend.

When a strong trend starts to correct up but there is no strong bull spike and market stays below the moving average, the always-in position might remain short. Look for sell rallies and low 1/low 2 setups.

## Time Frame Considerations

- 15-minute chart has fewer reversals than 5-minute chart
- 10,000 tick chart has even fewer reversals
- Reduce decision frequency by using higher time frames or larger tick bars

## Early Day Trading

Look for always-in trades early in the day because they often result in profitable swing trades. Sometimes just a single strong trend bar is required to establish the always-in direction.

## Key Rule

> "Do not take reversal trades until the always-in direction flips."

This prevents fighting the trend and improves probability of success.
