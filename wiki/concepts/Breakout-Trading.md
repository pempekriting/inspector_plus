# Breakout Trading

Every trend bar is a breakout that might succeed or fail and lead to a reversal. The core skill in trading is assessing whether a breakout will succeed or fail.

## Breakout Success vs Failure

- **Successful Breakout**: Follow-through in the direction of the breakout, creating a new trend or extending the current one
- **Failed Breakout**: Reversal back through the breakout level, trapping traders who entered on the breakout

## Breakout Types

### Trend Breakout

A bar that breaks above a prior high or below a prior low in the direction of the trend. Usually followed by at least a small pullback (bull flag/bear flag).

### Reversal Breakout

A breakout in the opposite direction of the trend. This is what creates reversals - a countertrend breakout that succeeds.

### Trading Range Breakout

Breakout from a trading range (support/resistance). Most breakout attempts in a trading range fail, which is why expanding triangles form.

## Failed Breakout Trading

When a breakout fails and reverses, it creates trading opportunities:

- **Failed Bear Breakout**: Price breaks below support but reverses up - buy signal
- **Failed Bull Breakout**: Price breaks above resistance but reverses down - sell signal

## Breakout Pullback

After a successful breakout, price often pulls back to test the breakout level before continuing in the direction of the breakout. This creates a second entry opportunity.

## Breakout vs Retest

Some breakouts are followed by a test (retest of old level), while others go straight through. A test of the old level often acts as support/resistance.

## Key Principles

1. **Most Breakouts Fail**: Especially in trading ranges, most breakout attempts fail
2. **Wait for Confirmation**: Do not assume a breakout will succeed - wait for follow-through
3. **Look for Momentum**: Strong breakouts have follow-through bars in the direction of the breakout
4. **Channel Breakouts**: Breakouts of steep channels often fail and reverse

## Institutional Impact

Institutional buy and sell programs generate most of the volume. They trade at support and resistance levels based on math and logic. Understanding this helps predict where breakouts might succeed or fail.
