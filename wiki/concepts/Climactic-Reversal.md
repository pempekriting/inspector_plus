# Climactic Reversal

A spike followed by a spike in the opposite direction. Also called V-top or V-bottom, though most V patterns have subtle price action showing hesitation rather than being perfect V shapes.

## The Core Mechanism

> "The market is always trying to break out, and then the market tries to make every breakout fail."

Every trend bar is a breakout that might succeed or fail and lead to a reversal. A climactic reversal is simply a breakout and then a failed breakout.

## Buy Climax Sequence

1. **Spike Up**: Strong bulls keep buying because they believe market will go higher
2. **Profit Taking Window**: At some point, strong bulls know market has gone too far, too fast. They seize the brief moment to sell out longs.
3. **Aggressive Selling**: Their profit taking combined with institutional short selling overwhelms buyers
4. **Spike Down**: Market reverses sharply - the climax

## Sell Climax Sequence

Same pattern in opposite direction - a sharp sell-off followed by a spike up.

## Channel After Climax

After a climactic reversal (spike up then spike down, or vice versa), there will often be a channel that fails, then the market forms a trading range as bulls and bears fight to establish a channel direction.

The channel direction can be unclear for a while because there are spikes in both directions.

## Trading Climaxes

- **Most Reversals Fail**: About 80% of reversal attempts fail. Institutions constantly try to reverse trends but fail most of the time.
- **Spike Pullback vs Spike Reversal**: A spike pullback (in direction of trend) does not need to get tested since traders agree trend is resuming. A spike reversal (countertrend) usually gets tested because traders are less willing to believe a reversal will succeed.
- **Countertrend Traders**: They draw trend channel lines hoping for overshoot and reversal. When channel is steep, it is a losing strategy to buy every reversal from channel overshoot.

## Examples

- **Outside Up Bar**: A bar that reverses a sell-off quickly. Many bulls looking to buy the failed low become paralyzed - instead they face a quick decision at what could be the top of a bear flag.
- **Inverted V Top**: A climactic opening reversal that breaks out of the top of a small bear trend channel line.

## Key Insight

> "The better traders become at assessing whether a breakout will succeed or fail, the better positioned they are to make a living at trading."
